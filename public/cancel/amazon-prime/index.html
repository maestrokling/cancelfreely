Cancellation Steps

  1. Go to amazon.com and sign in.
  2. Go to Account & Lists > Prime > Manage Membership, or go directly to amazon.com/mc.
  3. Click 'Update, cancel and more' then select 'End Membership'. Amazon will present multiple intermediate screens before reaching the final confirmation.
  4. Navigate through the retention screens — Amazon will offer a discounted rate, a pause, and remind you of benefits before showing the final cancel button. The final confirmation button is 'End My Benefits' — not labeled 'Cancel' which is a documented dark pattern.

⚠ Watch Out — Dark Patterns

⚠ multi-screen cancellation maze (Iliad flow)

Amazon's Prime cancellation was the subject of an FTC lawsuit and $2.5B settlement in 2025. The cancellation flow was designed to maximize clicks and confusion, with the final cancel button labeled 'End My Benefits' rather than 'Cancel'. The FTC called this the 'Iliad Flow' internally at Amazon.

⚠ free trial auto-enrollment

Amazon's Prime free trial auto-enrolled users into paid memberships. The $2.5B settlement required Amazon to change enrollment and cancellation flows.

What to Expect (Retention Tactics)

If you contact support, they will likely offer:

  • Offers a pause (1-3 months) instead of canceling.
  • Offers a discounted Prime rate.
  • Shows all Prime benefits you'll lose (shipping, video, music, photos, etc.).

You do not need to accept any of these. Say: "I would like to cancel my account completely." Repeat as needed. You do not need to provide a reason.
