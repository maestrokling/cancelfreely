Cancellation Steps

  1. Go to nytimes.com and sign in.
  2. Click your account name, then go to 'Account' > 'Manage Account'.
  3. Under your subscription, look for 'Cancel Subscription'. The cancel option may not be visible for all subscription types. Some users are routed to a chat or phone flow instead.
  4. If online cancellation is not available, call 1-800-698-4637 or use the live chat at help.nytimes.com. Phone/chat agents will make retention offers before completing cancellation.
  5. If subscribed via Apple, Amazon, or Google Play, cancel through those platforms instead.

⚠ Watch Out — Dark Patterns

⚠ online cancellation suppression

NYT does not offer online cancellation for all subscription types. Many users are routed to phone or chat, where they face retention pressure. This has been widely documented as a deliberate dark pattern.

⚠ introductory pricing trap

NYT heavily promotes introductory rates ($1-4/week for first year) that auto-renew at full price ($17-25+/week). Many users cancel after the intro period expires.

What to Expect (Retention Tactics)

If you contact support, they will likely offer:

  • Phone/chat agents offer significant discounts (often 50-75% off) to stay.
  • Offers a pause on delivery or digital access.
  • Reminds you of Games, Cooking, Wirecutter access bundled in.

You do not need to accept any of these. Say: "I would like to cancel my account completely." Repeat as needed. You do not need to provide a reason.
