Cancellation Steps

  1. TSA PreCheck is not a recurring subscription. It is a 5-year enrollment costing $78 ($70 online renewal). To not renew, simply do nothing when it expires. There is nothing to actively cancel -- your enrollment simply lapses after 5 years.
  2. If you enrolled through an enrollment provider (IDEMIA, Telos, Alclear, etc.) and they enabled auto-renewal, log into that provider's portal to disable auto-renewal. IDEMIA: universalenroll.dhs.gov/myaccount. Check your enrollment confirmation email for your provider.
  3. Renewal notices arrive by email approximately 6 months before expiration. Ignore them if you do not wish to renew. These are legitimate TSA/DHS emails, not spam, but they do not auto-charge you unless you opted into auto-renewal.
  4. If you were auto-charged for a renewal you did not authorize, contact your enrollment provider to dispute and request a refund. TSA itself does not process payments -- enrollment providers handle billing.

What to Expect (Retention Tactics)

If you contact support, they will likely offer:

  • Sends multiple renewal reminder emails starting 6 months before expiration to create urgency around re-enrollment.
  • Some enrollment providers default to auto-renewal opt-in during the initial enrollment process.

You do not need to accept any of these. Say: "I would like to cancel my account completely." Repeat as needed. You do not need to provide a reason.
