Cancellation Steps

  1. Determine your plan type: out-of-pocket (self-pay), insurance-covered, or employer/EAP benefits. The cancellation path differs for each. Out-of-pocket plans are the most straightforward to cancel online.
  2. For out-of-pocket plans: Go to talkspace.com or open the Talkspace app, sign in, and navigate to account settings.
  3. Select 'Manage Subscription' or 'Cancel Plan'. Talkspace will offer a pause option before full cancellation. You must click past the pause offer to reach full cancellation.
  4. For insurance or employer-covered plans: visit help.talkspace.com or contact Talkspace support for plan-specific cancellation instructions, as these are managed differently. Insurance-covered plans may require contacting your insurance provider or HR department instead.
  5. Confirm cancellation. No refund is provided for unused time in the current billing period. Your therapy chat history remains accessible for a limited time after cancellation.

⚠ Watch Out — Dark Patterns

What to Expect (Retention Tactics)

If you contact support, they will likely offer:

  • Offers a pause option (typically 1-3 months) before allowing full cancellation.
  • May offer a reduced rate or therapist change if dissatisfaction is cited as the reason for leaving.

You do not need to accept any of these. Say: "I would like to cancel my account completely." Repeat as needed. You do not need to provide a reason.

Free Alternatives

  • SAMHSA National Helpline — Free 24/7 helpline: 1-800-662-4357 for mental health and substance abuse referrals
  • 7 Cups (free) — Free emotional support through trained volunteer listeners
Notes: Talkspace plans range from approximately $69-$109/week for self-pay messaging therapy, with live sessions costing more. Insurance coverage varies by plan and state. The cancellation process is relatively clean for self-pay users but becomes significantly more complex for insurance or employer-sponsored plans, where Talkspace may direct you back to your benefits administrator. No prorated refunds are given for unused time within a billing period. Talkspace is publicly traded (TALK) and has faced scrutiny over therapist working conditions and data privacy practices.
