Cancellation Steps

  1. Go to youtube.com/paid_memberships and sign in with the Google account that has the subscription. On mobile: open the YouTube app > tap your profile picture > Paid memberships. If subscribed via Apple, cancel through iOS Settings > Subscriptions instead.
  2. Find YouTube Premium and click 'Manage'. If you have a family plan, only the family manager can cancel.
  3. Click 'Cancel membership' or 'Deactivate'. Google will present retention offers including a pause option and possibly a discounted rate.
  4. Navigate through the retention screens and confirm cancellation. Access continues through the end of the billing period. Ads return after the subscription expires. Downloaded videos for offline viewing are removed.

⚠ Watch Out — Dark Patterns

⚠ multi-step retention gauntlet

Google presents multiple retention screens during cancellation including pause offers, plan switches, and benefit reminders, requiring several clicks to reach the actual cancellation confirmation.

⚠ loss aversion with usage stats

What to Expect (Retention Tactics)

If you contact support, they will likely offer:

  • Offers a 1-3 month pause option before allowing full cancellation.
  • May present a discounted rate (often for 3 months) to users who proceed past the pause offer.
  • Shows an 'ads you avoided' counter to emphasize the value of ad-free viewing.

You do not need to accept any of these. Say: "I would like to cancel my account completely." Repeat as needed. You do not need to provide a reason.

Free Alternatives

  • YouTube (free with ads) — Full YouTube access with ads; no background play or offline downloads
  • uBlock Origin (browser extension) — Free ad blocker for desktop browsers (does not work on mobile apps)
  • NewPipe (Android, free) — Free open-source Android YouTube client with background play and no ads
Notes: YouTube Premium costs $13.99/month for Individual or $22.99/month for Family (up to 5 members), with annual plans available at a discount. The price increased from $11.99 to $13.99 in 2023. The subscription includes ad-free YouTube, YouTube Music Premium, background play on mobile, and offline video downloads. Google has progressively made the free YouTube experience more ad-heavy, with longer and more frequent ad breaks, which many view as a deliberate strategy to push users toward Premium. The cancellation flow has become more aggressive with retention screens compared to earlier years. YouTube Music Premium ($10.99/month) is a separate, cheaper option if you only want ad-free music.
