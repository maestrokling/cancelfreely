How to Cancel Wall Street Journal

Difficult (CFS 4/5)
Method: phone or chat · Last verified: 2025-09-13 · Official site

Cancellation Steps

  1. Call WSJ customer service: 1-800-JOURNAL (1-800-568-7625). Online self-service cancellation is not available for most subscribers. WSJ requires phone or chat for cancellation — this has been widely reported as an intentional dark pattern.
  2. Alternatively, use the live chat at wsj.com — go to Help > Contact Us > Chat. Chat availability varies; phone is more reliable.
  3. State clearly that you want to cancel. Agents will make multiple retention offers. Be direct: 'I want to cancel my subscription completely, effective immediately.'
  4. In a small number of US states (CA, VT, and others with strong consumer protection laws), online cancellation may be available. Check your account settings first — if a cancel option is visible, use it.

Dark Patterns

  • phone-only cancellation: WSJ requires phone or chat to cancel for most subscribers despite having a fully online signup process. This is one of the most documented dark patterns in news subscriptions, flagged repeatedly on Reddit r/assholedesign.
  • state-dependent online access: Online cancellation is only available in certain states with consumer protection laws that mandate it (e.g., California). Users in other states are forced to call.

Retention Tactics

  • Phone agents offer significant discounts (often 50%+ off) to retain subscribers.
  • Offers a temporary pause on billing.

Free Alternatives

  • Reuters — Free financial and business news
  • Bloomberg (limited free) — Limited free articles per month
  • Public libraries — Some libraries offer WSJ digital access
