How to Cancel AT&T

Difficult (CFS 4/5)
Method: phone · Last verified: 2026-03-03 · Official site

Cancellation Steps

  1. Call AT&T: 1-800-288-2020. Say 'cancel service' when prompted. AT&T does not offer online self-service cancellation for most accounts. Phone is the primary path.
  2. Verify your account and state clearly you want to cancel. Retention agents will make offers. Be direct and repeat your intent to cancel.
  3. IMPORTANT — check for Early Termination Fees (ETF): AT&T Fiber has no ETF. Wireless lines on installment plans require you to pay off the device balance. Older 2-year contract plans carry an ETF. If you have a device installment plan, you still owe the remaining device balance regardless of service cancellation.
  4. Return all AT&T equipment (gateways, set-top boxes) to a UPS Store within 21 days at no charge. Keep the UPS tracking receipt.

Dark Patterns

  • device installment obligation confusion: AT&T separates device installment plans from service plans. Canceling service does not cancel the device payment obligation. Many customers are surprised to find they still owe hundreds of dollars on a phone after canceling service.
  • phone-only cancellation: Online self-service cancellation is not available. All cancellations require calling, where retention agents are deployed.

Retention Tactics

  • Offers bill credits or plan discounts.
  • Offers to match competitor pricing.
  • Reminds you of device payment obligations remaining.

Free Alternatives

  • T-Mobile — Frequently runs switching promos covering device payoffs
  • Mint Mobile — Budget wireless on T-Mobile network
Notes: AT&T Fiber (internet) has no early termination fee — a genuine advantage over older cable contracts. Wireless cancellation is complicated if you have a device installment plan — you must pay off the remaining device balance. AT&T runs switching promotions for competitors, so check T-Mobile/Verizon for trade-in and payoff offers before canceling.
