Cancellation Steps

  1. Sign into your Slack workspace on desktop at slack.com. You must be a Workspace Owner or the person who originally upgraded the workspace. Workspace Admins cannot cancel.
  2. Click your workspace name in the top left, then select 'Tools & settings' > 'Workspace settings'. This opens in a browser window even if you started from the desktop app.
  3. Go to the 'Billing' tab.
  4. Select 'Cancel plan' or 'Switch to Free' to downgrade to the free tier. On annual plans, unused months may be refunded pro-rata depending on your agreement. On monthly plans, access continues through the end of the billing period.
  5. Confirm the downgrade. Your workspace reverts to the free plan with 90-day message history. Messages older than 90 days become hidden (not deleted) on the free plan. Re-upgrading restores full history.

⚠ Watch Out — Dark Patterns

⚠ data loss anxiety

Warns that you will lose access to message history beyond 90 days, creating urgency around potential data loss even though messages are retained and restored upon re-upgrading.

⚠ buried cancellation path

What to Expect (Retention Tactics)

If you contact support, they will likely offer:

  • May offer a discounted plan or extended trial period when cancellation is initiated.
  • Prominently displays a summary of workspace activity and integrations that will be affected by downgrading.

You do not need to accept any of these. Say: "I would like to cancel my account completely." Repeat as needed. You do not need to provide a reason.

Free Alternatives

  • Slack (free tier) — Free tier with 90-day message history, 10 app integrations, and 1:1 huddles
  • Discord (free) — Free team communication with unlimited message history, voice channels, and screen sharing
  • Mattermost (free, self-hosted) — Open-source Slack alternative with unlimited history when self-hosted
Notes: Slack Pro costs $8.75/user/month (billed annually) or $10.50/user/month (billed monthly), with Business+ at $15/user/month. The free tier is functional for small teams but the 90-day message history limit is the primary pain point driving upgrades. Cancellation requires Workspace Owner permissions, which can be a blocker in organizations where ownership has changed hands or the original upgrader has left. Slack was acquired by Salesforce in 2021, and enterprise contracts may have different cancellation terms. Pro-rata refunds on annual plans are handled case-by-case through Slack support.
